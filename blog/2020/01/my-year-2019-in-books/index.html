My Year 2019 in Books

Thursday, January 2, 2020 2 minutes read
Table of contents
  • My 2019 in numbers
  • My TOP 10 read books in 2019

Any Goodreads user has access to a yearly report with some statistics and basically the covers of all the books read in one year. In order to have it the user only has to set the books as read and the read date to any time in that year. Taking advantage of this nice feature I will summarize My 2019 in Books from Goodreads, as I did one year ago for my read books in 2018.

My 2019 in numbers

  • I read 7,878 pages across 57 books, a 114% of my 50 books read in 2019 goal
  • That’s 20 novels (not taking into account comics or short stories)
  • The average length was 138 pages
  • My average rating was 3.6 (up to 5)
  • The longest book I read was my nth reading of The Lord of the Rings, by J.R.R. Tolkien

The featured image goes to Nnedi Okorafor, as my main discovery of the year after reading her Binti trilogy. She has won a Hugo, a Nebula, a World Fantasy Award and a Locus Award.

I’m not copying here the full list, friend me on Goodreads if you are curious, but at least I want to highlight some of them.

My TOP 10 read books in 2019

  1. The Lord of the Rings by J.R.R. Tolkien
  2. Binti by Nnedi Okorafor
  3. El paciente by Juan Gómez Jurado (you can read my review in Spanish in Goodreads)
  4. Los girasoles ciegos by Alberto Méndez
  5. Loba Negra by Juan Gómez Jurado
  6. The Problems of Philosophy by Bertrand Russell
  7. The Night Masquerade by Nnedi Okorafor
  8. Sumalee: Historias de Trakaul by Javier Salazar Calle (you can read my review in Spanish in Goodreads)
  9. The Machine Stops by E.M. Forster
  10. Childhood’s End by Arthur C. Clarke
