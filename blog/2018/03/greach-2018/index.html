Greach 2018

March 20, 2018 LuisGC

2 minute read

Greach 2018

Some days ago I attended, as usual, to the fantastic Greach Conference, an international conference about the Apache Groovy language and ecosystem: Groovy, Grails, Gradle,… Greach is held each year in Madrid but everything is in English, and nowadays it’s surely one of the Top worldwide events about this technologies.

Greach is a non-profitable event is organized by Iván López (@ilopar) and Alberto Vilches (@albertovilches), with some help from colleagues and a lot of sponsors.

TLDR; The conference content and speakers were great, in addition to the logistics. I couldn’t attend the workshop day (my fault), and it was a pity specially because this year I had paid for it. Apart from learning (as usual), this year was specially exciting with the worldwide announcement of Micronaut.

Let me summarize some of the talks I attended:

Talks

Groovy Keynote: 2.5+ Roadmap, by Paul King

Building DSL using Groovy, by Puneet Behl

I’ve seen Grails code you wouldn’t believe…, by Iván López

Groovy GString magic, by Jacob Aae Mikkelsen

From Functions to Monadic Style, by Dierk König

Mapping a tree with Grails, by Sergio del Amo

Reactive All The Way Down with Ratpack, RxGroovy, React, and RabbitMq, by Steve Pember

Launching the Micro Future: Groovy, Grails and MicroNaut, by Graeme Rocher

Reactive Microservices with MicroNaut, by Álvaro Sánchez-Mariscal

GORM Reloaded – Data Services for the Win, by Graeme Rocher

101 scripts that can save you the day, by Jorge Aguilera and Miguel Ángel Rueda

DevOps Glue – Task Automation with Gitlab-CI and Groovy, by Kevin Wittek

Surviving in a Microservices Environment, by Steve Pember
